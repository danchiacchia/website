About Daniel

Multi-method research on communication in hard moments

Daniel J. Chiacchia is a PhD Candidate in Organizational Behaviour and HR Management at the Rotman School of Management, University of Toronto. Drawing on social psychological and micro-organizational behavior theory, his research examines the role of emotions, norms, and authenticity in how we communicate through adversity — with implications spanning organizational behavior, psychology, marketing, communication, and health.

His multi-method approach combines qualitative interviews, lab and field experiments, archival analyses, and psychometric natural language processing to capture nuanced expression at scale. He is committed to open science: pre-registering studies, powering experiments appropriately, internally replicating effects, and making data, code, and stimuli publicly available.
